## Key insights from this session

1. The project's center of gravity shifted from "conducted performance instrument" to "prosthetic inner life for collaborative work with LLMs."

2. The membrane work (admission tiers, anti-residue, evidence-driven promotion) is genuinely novel and potentially more interesting than the family taxonomy it protects.

3. Mueller's source code had discipline we'd collapsed. Reconstructing it was the right move. But now the question is whether the specifics (five families, coincidence-mark retrieval, the connection graph) are the right mechanisms for the collaborative case.

4. The Graffito miniworld 20-cycle result (6 autonomous reappraisal flips, developmental arc, adversity and recovery) is the strongest behavioral result so far. It shows the kernel producing path-dependent, non-collapsed dynamics on typed psychological material.

5. The project page is honest about being a foundation. "If later sessions do not become structurally different because of what earlier sessions discovered, the architecture does not earn its keep."

6. The three-level evaluation stack (mechanism integrity → behavioral dynamics → meaningful domain adequacy) was the right framework for not jumping from unit tests to "is it interesting?"

7. The question "what is this project actually for?" is now the most important question, ahead of any architecture or mechanism question.
