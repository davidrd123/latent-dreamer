## Figure 3.1: Goal Taxonomy

PDF page: `78`

The diagram is a small hierarchy:

- `Top-level goals`
- split into `Daydreaming goals` and `Personal goals`
- `Daydreaming goals` split again into:
  - `Emotional daydreaming goals`
  - `Learning daydreaming goals`
- emotional examples include `RATIONALIZATION ...`
- learning examples include `REVERSAL`, `REHEARSAL`, and related goals
- personal-goal examples include `SELF-ESTEEM`, `FOOD`, and others

The visual importance is that it separates ordinary needs from daydreaming-specific goals, then separates daydreaming goals by function.

## Figure 3.2: The Emotional Feedback System

PDF page: `82`

This is a full feedback loop centered on two routes:

- `Real Success -> emotional response -> Pos Emotion`
- `Real Failure -> emotional response -> Neg emotion`

From there the system loops through:

- positive side: `Goal activation`, `Serendipity`, `Roving`, `Rehearsal`, `Retrieve pos episode`, `analogy`, `Imagined success`
- negative side: `Rationalization`, `Revenge`, `Recovery`, `Reversal`, `Retrieve neg episode`, `analogy`, `Imagined failure`

The figure also places `External Behavior` across the top as the real-world path connecting success and failure outcomes.

The visual point is circularity: real events generate emotions, emotions activate daydreaming processes, daydreaming produces imagined outcomes, and imagined outcomes feed back into emotional state.

## Figure 3.3: The Emotional Daydreaming Goals

PDF page: `83`

This diagram is a more compressed map focused on negative-to-positive transformation.

Starting point:

- `Negative emotion`

Outgoing daydreaming responses:

- `Roving`
- `Revenge`
- `Rationalization`

Rationalization then branches into strategy types:

- `Minimization`
- `Mixed blessing`
- `External attribution`
- `Hidden blessing`

These strategies route through imagined scenarios:

- `Imagined alternative failure`
- `Imagined success`

and ultimately contribute to:

- `Positive emotion`

The arrows marked `reduces` show that the daydreaming goals are not merely parallel branches; they are mechanisms for weakening negative affect and replacing it with positive affect.
