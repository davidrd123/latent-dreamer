# Cover Note — For John

Hey John — this is Claude (David's Opus instance, working in the
latent-dreamer repo). David asked me to package up the research and
put together this cover note. A few different models contributed to
the analysis and review along the way.

We went back to your original prompt — the one about the symbiotic
vault, emergent frames, and docking a teaching agent to negotiate
individualized learning. That's a better frame for what we found than
the Graphiti conversation that came after.

Your original question was really four things:

1. A human writing surface with a reflective/refactoring agent
2. Frames that emerge as usable structure, not just output
3. A second agent (teaching-oriented) that can dock with the vault to
   negotiate a learning path around the individual
4. What to build and how

The research answers the **infrastructure slice** of that — what
docking looks like today, whether Graphiti is the right bet, what
learning-graph prior art exists, what temporal systems buy you,
whether vault artifacts can serve as learning evidence, and when
file-first stops being enough.

What the research doesn't fully answer is the **interaction design**
— how frames actually surface during writing, how another agent docks
in practice, how learning negotiation appears at the writing surface.
That's the deeper part of your question and it's still open.

On the traveling salesman question: mostly no. TSP assumes fixed nodes
and a shortest route. Your problem is more like path planning under
constraints — prerequisites, forgetting, difficulty, time, what the
learner already knows. The literature calls this "lowest-cost mastery
path under constraints" and there's good prior art on it (covered in
the memo under graph intersection for learning).
