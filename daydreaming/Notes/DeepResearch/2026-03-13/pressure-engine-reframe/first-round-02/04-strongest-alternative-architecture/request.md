## Prompt

```text
Assume the current `L1/L2/L3` decomposition may be wrong in one major way.

Construct the single strongest alternative architecture using adjacent traditions you think are relevant. Do not produce many alternatives; give the single strongest competitor.

Then compare it against the current architecture on:
- clarity of boundaries
- implementation tractability
- authoring burden
- explainability
- fit to the cited source lineages
- usefulness for early experiments

Finish by telling me what evidence or experiment would discriminate between the current architecture and your alternative.

Constraints:
- engage the supplied notes directly
- do not invent a speculative grand architecture unless it is genuinely stronger than the current one
- do not default to generic modularity advice

Output format:
- `Alternative architecture`
- `Comparison against current architecture`
- `Discriminating evidence or experiment`
- `Bottom line`
```
