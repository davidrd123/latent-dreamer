## Video format options

### Format A — narrated scroll (recommended)
- Screen record the combined HTML page (images + prose for 6 arc cycles)
- Narrate over it in plain terms
- ~60-90 seconds
- Pros: human voice explains why it matters, easy to produce
- Cons: requires recording + light editing

### Format B — silent auto-scroll with text cards
- HTML page auto-scrolls through 6 cycles with transitions
- Title card → cycles → end card
- Text-only explanation, no voiceover
- ~60-90 seconds
- Pros: zero recording needed, just screen capture
- Cons: viewer may not understand the significance without voice

### Format C — split screen
- Left: the generated images cycling slowly
- Right: the thought stream prose appearing
- Bottom: a thin bar showing Tony's regulation state climbing
- Narration over it
- ~90 seconds
- Pros: most visually compelling
- Cons: most work to produce

### Format D — keynote/slides with images
- Traditional slides: title → image+prose per cycle → end
- Can present live or record with voiceover
- Easiest to adjust last-minute
- Cons: less cinematic

## What the audience should take away

One thing: **this system's inner life is path-dependent.** What
happened earlier changes what the character thinks later. Not through
prompt engineering. Through a persistent cognitive engine with real
memory, real emotion, and real discipline about what earns trust.

That's different from "we gave an LLM a character sheet and it
generated nice prose." The engine decides what to think about. The
LLM inhabits the state it's given.
