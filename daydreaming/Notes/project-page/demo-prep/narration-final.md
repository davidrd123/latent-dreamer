## Reference: mechanical card-by-card notes (don't read aloud)

Internal detail for understanding, not for narration.

- C01: rationalization (meaning repair). Sensory load drops, agency
  rises slightly. Threat holds.
- C02: reversal (counterfactual replay). Sensory load rises, control
  falls. Threat holds.
- C06: rehearsal (embodied regulation). Entrainment 0.29→0.59,
  agency 0.52→0.70, control 0.49→0.73. First flip: threat→challenge.
- C08: reversal (street knockback). Agency/control fall. Flips back
  to threat.
- C13: rehearsal (deeper practice). Tony reaches flowing. Flips back
  to challenge.
- C20: reversal (street again). Pressure hits but gain holds.
  Challenge stays.

## The story in one paragraph

Tony starts overloaded, so the first winning move is rationalization:
making the situation feel more bearable. But when the mural and the
cops come forward, reversal takes over and the world still reads as
threat. The real shift comes when rehearsal wins in the apartment:
rhythm raises Tony's control and agency, and that changes the next
mural reading from threat to challenge. The street knocks him back
again, but later recoveries are stronger, and by the end the gain
holds.

---

## The grammar

- **Rationalization** = early meaning repair
- **Reversal** = threat/counterfactual pressure under street or
  mural stress
- **Rehearsal** = the real ratchet, because it changes Tony's
  carried regulation state

The arc is repeated knockback and stronger recovery, not a smooth
improvement curve.
